Grant the challenge when the questionnaire or voir dire record shows that the candidate cannot be impartial or cannot follow the court's instructions.  A candidate who says they would presume intent from a confession, refuse to credit a type of evidence the court allows, refuse to apply the stated burden of proof, or refuse to return a verdict the law permits is not fit to serve.  Deny the challenge when the record shows only a mild concern that the candidate can set aside and the candidate still commits to follow the law.
