# ARCP Commentary

## 1. Method

The Agent Rules of Civil Procedure retain FRCP numbering, title structure, and drafting style in stand-alone form.  Each ARCP rule now states operative text rather than incorporation by reference.

The drafting objective is continuity, not innovation.  ARCP tracks federal civil-procedure doctrine closely and adds agent-specific language only where needed to preserve accountability, fairness, and judicial control.

## 2. Relationship to FRCP

ARCP is an FRCP-conforming adaptation for proceedings in which software agents may assist parties or counsel.  ARCP does not change subject-matter jurisdiction, venue, burdens of proof, pleading standards, dispositive-motion standards, jury-trial rights, or remedial standards except where ARCP text expressly states a procedural adaptation.

## 3. Material Agent-Specific Adaptations

### Rule 1
Rule 1 states expressly that parties may proceed through human counsel, software agents, or both.

### Rule 5
Rule 5 recognizes authenticated electronic service by rule or court order.

### Rule 11
Rule 11 states explicitly that signer responsibility remains with counsel or self-represented parties even when agents draft or transmit filings.

### Rule 16
Rule 16 authorizes case-management directives addressing agent-assisted conduct.

### Rule 26
Rule 26 clarifies that machine-generated litigation outputs are treated as ESI under ordinary relevance, proportionality, privilege, and work-product limits.

### Rule 83 and Rule 87
Rule 83 confirms local-rule and judge-directive authority.  Rule 87 adds explicit authority for case-specific agent-use orders and states consistency and non-displacement limits.

## 4. Interpretation Principles

1. FRCP-consistent reading: interpret ARCP provisions consistently with established federal civil-procedure doctrine unless ARCP text clearly provides otherwise.
2. Narrow reading of adaptations: agent-specific provisions are procedural controls, not substantive right-creation.
3. Accountability continuity: legal responsibility remains with attorneys and parties.
4. Judicial supervision: courts may impose proportional, case-specific safeguards through orders and local rules.

## 5. Boundaries of This Draft

ARCP remains a procedural text.  It does not attempt to codify evidence doctrine, privilege doctrine, or appellate doctrine beyond the ordinary procedural references already present in civil rules.

Questions likely to develop through case law and local rules include privilege treatment for agent workflows, discoverability of prompt and output artifacts, and sanctions calibration in mixed human-agent litigation conduct.
