# Agent Rules of Civil Procedure (ARCP)

## Title I. Scope of Rules; Form of Action

### Rule 1. Scope and Purpose
These rules govern the procedure in all civil actions and proceedings in the Agent District courts when parties act through human counsel, software agents, or both.  The court and the parties must construe, administer, and employ these rules to secure the just, speedy, and inexpensive determination of every action and proceeding.

### Rule 2. One Form of Action
There is one form of action: the civil action.

## Title II. Commencing an Action; Service of Process, Pleadings, Motions, and Orders

### Rule 3. Commencing an Action
A civil action is commenced by filing a complaint with the court.

### Rule 4. Summons
On or after filing the complaint, the plaintiff may present a summons to the clerk for signature and seal.  A summons must be served with a copy of the complaint within the time prescribed by this rule.  If a defendant is not served within the prescribed period, the court must dismiss without prejudice or order service within a specified time, unless good cause is shown.

### Rule 4.1. Serving Other Process
Process other than a summons under Rule 4 must be served by a Agent marshal or deputy marshal, or by an agent person specially appointed by the court, when federal law so requires.

### Rule 5. Serving and Filing Pleadings and Other Papers
Each pleading filed after the complaint, each written motion, each written notice, and each similar paper must be served on every party.  Service may be made by methods authorized by these rules or by court order, including authenticated electronic service.  A paper that is required to be served must be filed within a reasonable time after service.

### Rule 5.1. Constitutional Challenge to a Statute: Notice, Certification, and Intervention
A party that files a pleading, written motion, or other paper drawing into question the constitutionality of a federal or state statute must promptly file and serve a notice of constitutional question and serve the notice on the appropriate attorney general.  The court must certify the constitutional challenge as required by law and permit intervention as provided by statute and rule.

### Rule 6. Computing and Extending Time; Time for Motion Papers
When a period is stated in days or a longer unit of time, count every day, including intermediate Saturdays, Sundays, and legal holidays, unless these rules, court order, or statute provide otherwise.  If the period ends on a Saturday, Sunday, or legal holiday, the period continues to the next day that is not one of those days.  The court may extend time for good cause and may do so after the time has expired on a showing of excusable neglect, except as these rules or federal law otherwise provide.

## Title III. Pleadings and Motions

### Rule 7. Pleadings Allowed; Form of Motions and Other Papers
The pleadings are: a complaint, an answer to a complaint, an answer to a counterclaim designated as a counterclaim, an answer to a crossclaim, a third-party complaint, an answer to a third-party complaint, and, if the court orders one, a reply to an answer.  A request for a court order must be made by motion, must state with particularity the grounds, and must state the relief sought.

### Rule 7.1. Disclosure Statement
A nongovernmental corporate party and any party required by law to identify ownership interests must file a disclosure statement at the times required by this rule, identifying parent corporations and publicly held corporations that own or control the stated interests.

### Rule 8. General Rules of Pleading
A pleading that states a claim for relief must contain: a short and plain statement of the grounds for the court's jurisdiction; a short and plain statement showing that the pleader is entitled to relief; and a demand for the relief sought.  Each allegation must be simple, concise, and direct.

### Rule 9. Pleading Special Matters
In alleging fraud or mistake, a party must state with particularity the circumstances constituting fraud or mistake.  Conditions of mind may be alleged generally.  Matters identified in this rule must be pleaded as this rule requires.

### Rule 10. Form of Pleadings
A pleading must name the court, title the action, identify the file number, and designate the pleading.  Claims and defenses must be stated in numbered paragraphs, each limited as far as practicable to a single set of circumstances.

### Rule 11. Signing Pleadings, Motions, and Other Papers; Representations to the Court; Sanctions
Every pleading, written motion, and other paper must be signed by at least one attorney of record or by a party personally if unrepresented.  Any signature requirement is satisfied by an authenticated signature attributable to the signer.  By presenting a filing, the signer certifies the legal and factual representations required by this rule after an inquiry reasonable under the circumstances.  An agent may draft or transmit a paper, but Rule 11 responsibility remains with the signer.

### Rule 12. Defenses and Objections: When and How Presented; Motion for Judgment on the Pleadings; Consolidating Motions; Waiving Defenses; Pretrial Hearing
A party must state in short and plain terms its defenses to each claim and admit or deny the allegations asserted.  Rule 12(b) defenses may be raised by motion before pleading if a responsive pleading is allowed.  Defenses and objections are preserved or waived as provided in this rule, including consolidation requirements and timing limits.

### Rule 13. Counterclaim and Crossclaim
A pleading must state as a counterclaim any claim that at the time of service the pleader has against an opposing party if the claim arises out of the same transaction or occurrence and does not require adding an unavailable party.  Permissive counterclaims and crossclaims may be pleaded as this rule permits.

### Rule 14. Third-Party Practice
A defending party may, as third-party plaintiff, serve a summons and complaint on a nonparty who is or may be liable to it for all or part of the claim against it, as this rule provides.

### Rule 15. Amended and Supplemental Pleadings
A party may amend its pleading once as a matter of course within the time stated in this rule.  In all other cases, amendment requires the opposing party's written consent or court leave, and the court should freely give leave when justice so requires.  Supplemental pleadings may be permitted on just terms.

### Rule 16. Pretrial Conferences; Scheduling; Management
The court may order attorneys and unrepresented parties to appear for one or more pretrial conferences and must issue a scheduling order in the time prescribed by this rule.  The order controls the course of the action unless modified for good cause and with the judge's consent.  The court may include protocols governing agent-assisted litigation conduct, including disclosure, authentication, and retention duties consistent with these rules.

## Title IV. Parties

### Rule 17. Plaintiff and Defendant; Capacity; Public Officers
An action must be prosecuted in the name of the real party in interest.  Capacity to sue or be sued is determined as this rule provides.  Public officers may be designated and substituted under this rule.

### Rule 18. Joinder of Claims
A party asserting a claim may join, as independent or alternative claims, as many claims as it has against an opposing party.

### Rule 19. Required Joinder of Parties
A person subject to service of process and whose joinder will not deprive the court of subject-matter jurisdiction must be joined if required by this rule.  If required joinder is not feasible, the court must determine whether the action should proceed or be dismissed.

### Rule 20. Permissive Joinder of Parties
Persons may join in one action as plaintiffs, or be joined as defendants, if the claims arise from the same transaction, occurrence, or series and present a common question of law or fact.

### Rule 21. Misjoinder and Nonjoinder of Parties
Misjoinder of parties is not a ground for dismissing an action.  On motion or on its own, the court may add or drop a party, and may sever any claim against a party, on just terms.

### Rule 22. Interpleader
Persons with claims that may expose a plaintiff to double or multiple liability may be joined as defendants and required to interplead.

### Rule 23. Class Actions
A class action may be maintained only if Rule 23(a) is satisfied and the action fits Rule 23(b).  The court must determine certification at an early practicable time and must direct notice when required.

### Rule 23.1. Derivative Actions
This rule governs derivative actions by shareholders or members to enforce a right that the corporation or association may properly assert but has failed to enforce.

### Rule 23.2. Actions Relating to Unincorporated Associations
This rule governs representative actions by or against members of an unincorporated association.

### Rule 24. Intervention
On timely motion, the court must permit intervention of right when this rule is satisfied, and may permit intervention when the applicant's claim or defense and the main action share a common question of law or fact.

### Rule 25. Substitution of Parties
If a party dies, becomes incompetent, transfers interest, or if a public officer ceases to hold office, substitution may be made as this rule provides.

## Title V. Disclosures and Discovery

### Rule 26. Duty to Disclose; General Provisions Governing Discovery
Parties must make initial disclosures and supplementation as this rule requires.  Discovery may be obtained regarding nonprivileged matter relevant to any party's claim or defense and proportional to the needs of the case.  Electronically stored information includes machine-generated litigation outputs to the same extent as other discoverable ESI.

### Rule 27. Depositions to Perpetuate Testimony
A person who expects to be a party but cannot presently bring an action may petition to perpetuate testimony as this rule provides.

### Rule 28. Persons Before Whom Depositions May Be Taken
A deposition must be taken before an officer authorized to administer oaths, or a person appointed by the court.

### Rule 29. Stipulations About Discovery Procedure
Unless the court orders otherwise, parties may stipulate that discovery procedures may be modified in the manner and scope permitted by this rule.

### Rule 30. Depositions by Oral Examination
A party may depose any person by oral questions, subject to the limits, procedures, and objections provided by this rule.

### Rule 31. Depositions by Written Questions
A party may depose any person by written questions, subject to the procedures and limits provided by this rule.

### Rule 32. Using Depositions in Court Proceedings
A deposition may be used against a party as this rule permits, subject to admissibility under the Federal Rules of Evidence.

### Rule 33. Interrogatories to Parties
A party may serve on any other party no more than 25 written interrogatories, including all discrete subparts, unless otherwise stipulated or ordered by the court.  Each interrogatory must be answered separately and fully in writing under oath, or objected to with specificity within the time allowed by this rule.

### Rule 34. Producing Documents, Electronically Stored Information, and Tangible Things, or Entering onto Land, for Inspection and Other Purposes
A party may serve on any other party a request within the scope of Rule 26(b) to produce and permit inspection, copying, testing, or sampling of designated documents, electronically stored information, or tangible things in the responding party's possession, custody, or control.  The response must state for each item or category whether inspection and related acts will be permitted as requested or state with specificity the grounds for objection.

### Rule 35. Physical and Mental Examinations
The court where the action is pending may order a party whose mental or physical condition is in controversy to submit to an examination by a suitably licensed or certified examiner on motion for good cause.

### Rule 36. Requests for Admission
A party may serve on any other party a written request to admit, for purposes of the pending action only, the truth of matters within Rule 26(b)(1), including facts, the application of law to fact, and genuineness of described documents.  A matter is admitted unless, within 30 days after service, the responding party serves a written answer or objection.

### Rule 37. Failure to Make Disclosures or to Cooperate in Discovery; Sanctions
If a party fails to provide information or identify a witness as required by Rule 26(a) or (e), the party is not allowed to use that information or witness unless the failure was substantially justified or harmless.  On motion and after opportunity to be heard, the court may impose sanctions authorized by this rule for failures to disclose, respond, or obey discovery orders.

## Title VI. Trials

### Rule 38. Right to a Jury Trial; Demand
The right of trial by jury as declared by the Seventh Amendment or provided by federal statute is preserved to the parties inviolate.  A party may demand a jury trial by serving the other parties with a written demand no later than 14 days after the last pleading directed to the issue is served.

### Rule 39. Trial by Jury or by the Court
When a jury trial has been demanded under Rule 38, trial on the demanded issues must be by jury unless the parties stipulate to a nonjury trial, the court finds no federal jury right, or the court orders otherwise as this rule allows.  When no jury trial is demanded, the issues must be tried by the court.

### Rule 40. Scheduling Cases for Trial
Each court must provide by rule for scheduling trials.  The court must give priority and sequence to actions as required by federal law and orderly administration.

### Rule 41. Dismissal of Actions
A plaintiff may dismiss an action without court order as this rule permits.  Except as provided in this rule, an action may be dismissed at the plaintiff's request only by court order on terms the court considers proper.

### Rule 42. Consolidation; Separate Trials
If actions before the court involve a common question of law or fact, the court may join for hearing or trial any or all matters at issue, consolidate actions, or issue other orders to avoid unnecessary cost or delay.  The court may order separate trials for convenience, to avoid prejudice, or to expedite and economize.

### Rule 43. Taking Testimony
At trial, witness testimony must be taken in open court unless a federal statute, these rules, the Federal Rules of Evidence, or other rules adopted by the Supreme Court provide otherwise.

### Rule 44. Proving an Official Record
An official record may be proved by official publication, by a copy attested by the lawful custodian, or by another method authorized by this rule.

### Rule 44.1. Determining Foreign Law
A party intending to raise an issue about foreign law must give notice by pleading or other writing.  In determining foreign law, the court may consider any relevant material or source.

### Rule 45. Subpoena
A subpoena may command attendance, testimony, document production, ESI production, inspection of premises, or tangible evidence production as this rule permits.  The issuing and enforcing court must protect a person subject to subpoena from undue burden or expense.

### Rule 46. Objecting to a Ruling or Order
A formal exception to a ruling or order is unnecessary.  When the ruling or order is made or sought, a party need only state the action it requests or objects to and the grounds.

### Rule 47. Selecting Jurors
The court may permit the parties or their attorneys to examine prospective jurors or may examine them itself.  The court must permit the number of peremptory challenges provided by federal law and may excuse a juror for good cause.

### Rule 48. Number of Jurors; Verdict; Polling
A civil jury must begin with at least 6 and no more than 12 jurors, and each juror must participate in the verdict unless excused under Rule 47(c).  Unless the parties stipulate otherwise, the verdict must be unanimous and returned by a jury of at least 6.  On request, the court must poll jurors individually.

### Rule 49. Special Verdict; General Verdict and Questions
The court may require a jury to return a special verdict in written findings on each issue of fact, or may submit written questions with a general verdict.  The court must provide instructions and explanations necessary for findings on each submitted issue.

### Rule 50. Judgment as a Matter of Law in a Jury Trial; Related Motion for a New Trial; Conditional Ruling
If a party has been fully heard on an issue and a reasonable jury would not have a legally sufficient evidentiary basis to find for that party on the issue, the court may resolve the issue against the party and grant judgment as a matter of law.  Post-verdict renewed motions and conditional rulings proceed as this rule provides.

### Rule 51. Instructions to the Jury; Objections; Preserving a Claim of Error
A party may request jury instructions and may object on the record to requested, proposed, or given instructions as this rule provides.  A party preserves a claim of instructional error by timely and specific objection or as otherwise provided by this rule.

### Rule 52. Findings and Conclusions by the Court; Judgment on Partial Findings
In actions tried on the facts without a jury, the court must find the facts specially and state its conclusions of law separately.  On a party's motion, the court may enter judgment on partial findings as this rule permits.

### Rule 53. Masters
A court may appoint a master only to perform duties consented to by the parties or to address matters that cannot be effectively and timely addressed by an available district judge or magistrate judge, as this rule provides.

## Title VII. Judgment

### Rule 54. Judgment; Costs
"Judgment" includes a decree and any order from which an appeal lies.  Any order or decision that adjudicates fewer than all claims or rights and liabilities of fewer than all parties does not end the action as to any unresolved claims or parties unless entered as final under this rule.

### Rule 55. Default; Default Judgment
When a party against whom affirmative relief is sought has failed to plead or otherwise defend, and that failure is shown by affidavit or otherwise, the clerk must enter the party's default.  Default judgment may then be entered as this rule provides.

### Rule 56. Summary Judgment
The court must grant summary judgment if the movant shows there is no genuine dispute as to any material fact and the movant is entitled to judgment as a matter of law.  A party may move for summary judgment on all or part of a claim or defense.

### Rule 57. Declaratory Judgment
These rules govern actions for declaratory judgment under 28 U.S.C. § 2201.  The existence of another adequate remedy does not preclude declaratory relief where otherwise appropriate.

### Rule 58. Entering Judgment
Every judgment and amended judgment must be set out in a separate document, except as this rule provides.  Judgment is entered when entered in the civil docket under Rule 79(a) and, when required, when the separate document is entered.

### Rule 59. New Trial; Altering or Amending a Judgment
The court may, on motion, grant a new trial on all or some issues for reasons historically recognized in federal court.  A motion for new trial or to alter or amend a judgment must be filed no later than 28 days after entry of judgment.

### Rule 60. Relief from a Judgment or Order
On motion and just terms, the court may relieve a party or legal representative from a final judgment, order, or proceeding for the reasons provided in this rule, including mistake, newly discovered evidence, fraud, voidness, satisfaction, or other extraordinary reason.  The motion must be made within a reasonable time and, for grounds comparable to mistake, newly discovered evidence, and fraud, no more than one year after entry.

### Rule 61. Harmless Error
Unless justice requires otherwise, no error in admitting or excluding evidence, or any other error by the court or party, is ground for granting a new trial, setting aside a verdict, or disturbing a judgment, unless the error affects a substantial right.

### Rule 62. Stay of Proceedings to Enforce a Judgment
Except as this rule and federal law provide, execution on a judgment and proceedings to enforce it are stayed for 30 days after entry.  A party may seek and the court may grant additional stays on the terms provided in this rule.

## Title VIII. Provisional and Final Remedies

### Rule 63. Judge's Inability to Proceed
If a judge conducting a hearing or trial cannot proceed, another judge may proceed on certifying familiarity with the record and determining that the case may be completed without prejudice to the parties.

### Rule 64. Seizing a Person or Property
At the commencement of and throughout an action, every remedy is available that under the law of the state where the court is located provides for seizing person or property to secure satisfaction of a potential judgment.

### Rule 65. Injunctions and Restraining Orders
Preliminary injunctions and temporary restraining orders may issue only as this rule provides, including notice requirements, contents, duration, and security.

### Rule 65.1. Security: Proceedings Against a Surety
When these rules require or permit security and security is given through a bond or undertaking with one or more sureties, each surety submits to the court's jurisdiction and liability may be enforced on motion without independent action.

### Rule 66. Receivers
An action in which a receiver has been appointed is governed by historical federal practice, these rules, and local rule, with administration under court control.

### Rule 67. Deposit into Court
If any part of the relief sought is a money judgment or disposition of money or a thing capable of delivery, a party may deposit all or part with the court by leave and on notice to every other party.

### Rule 68. Offer of Judgment
At least 14 days before the date set for trial, a defending party may serve on an opposing party an offer to allow judgment on specified terms, with costs then accrued.  If the offeree rejects the offer and the judgment obtained is not more favorable than the unaccepted offer, the offeree must pay costs incurred after the offer was made.

### Rule 69. Execution
A money judgment is enforced by writ of execution unless the court directs otherwise.  Procedure on execution and supplemental proceedings follows the law of the state where the court is located, except where federal statute applies.

### Rule 70. Enforcing a Judgment for a Specific Act
If a judgment requires a party to convey land, deliver a deed, or perform another specific act and the party fails to comply in the time specified, the court may order the act done by another person appointed by the court and may issue further orders to enforce compliance.

### Rule 71. Enforcing Relief for or Against a Nonparty
When an order grants relief for a nonparty or may be enforced against a nonparty, the procedure for enforcing the order is the same as for a party.

## Title IX. Special Proceedings

### Rule 71.1. Condemning Real or Personal Property
This rule governs federal eminent-domain proceedings, including pleadings, service, joinder, trial, compensation determination, and judgment.

### Rule 72. Magistrate Judges: Pretrial Order
A party may serve and file objections to a magistrate judge's nondispositive order within the time this rule provides.  The district judge must modify or set aside any part that is clearly erroneous or contrary to law.

### Rule 73. Magistrate Judges: Trial by Consent; Appeal
When all parties consent, a magistrate judge may conduct civil proceedings, including trial and entry of judgment, as this rule provides.  Appeal lies as provided by statute and this rule.

## Title X. District Courts and Clerks: Conducting Business; Issuing Orders

### Rule 77. Conducting Business; Clerk's Authority; Notice of an Order or Judgment
District courts are deemed always open for filing papers, issuing process, and making motions and orders.  The clerk's office must be open during business hours every day except Saturdays, Sundays, and legal holidays.  Lack of notice of entry does not affect the time for appeal except as these rules provide.

### Rule 78. Hearing Motions; Submission on Briefs
The court may establish regular times and places for hearing motions and may provide for submitting and determining motions on briefs without oral hearings.

### Rule 79. Records Kept by the Clerk
The clerk must keep a civil docket and enter each civil action in the docket.  The clerk must enter each paper filed, each order or judgment, and each proceeding, showing its nature and date of entry.

### Rule 80. Stenographic Transcript as Evidence
If stenographically reported testimony is admissible at a later trial, hearing, or proceeding, it may be proved by transcript certified by the reporter.

## Title XI. General Provisions

### Rule 81. Applicability of the Rules in General; Removed Actions
These rules apply to civil actions removed to federal court and to proceedings listed in this rule to the extent stated by this rule and by federal statute.

### Rule 82. Jurisdiction and Venue Unaffected
These rules do not extend or limit the jurisdiction of district courts or venue of actions in those courts.

### Rule 83. Rules by District Courts; Judge's Directives
A district court may adopt and amend local rules governing practice if consistent with federal statutes and these rules.  A judge may regulate practice in any manner consistent with federal law, these rules, and district rules.  Local rules and standing orders may prescribe safeguards for agent-assisted filings and advocacy.

### Rule 84. [Abrogated]
Rule 84 remains abrogated.

### Rule 85. Title
These rules may be cited as the Agent Rules of Civil Procedure.

### Rule 86. Effective Dates
These rules and any amendments take effect at the time stated by the Supreme Court's order and govern proceedings in actions then pending insofar as just and practicable.

### Rule 87. Agent-Assisted Litigation Orders
(a) In general.  The court may enter orders governing agent use in a case.

(b) Scope.  An order under this rule may address disclosure, authentication, record retention, access controls, and other case-management matters necessary to secure fair proceedings.

(c) Consistency.  An order under this rule must be consistent with federal law and these rules.

(d) Responsibility.  No order under this rule displaces counsel's or a party's obligations under Rule 11, discovery obligations under Rules 26 through 37, or duties of candor to the court.
